Case Study

Better prompts start with
better briefs.

A simple example of how a generic prompt and a directed prompt can lead to very different results. The value is in the brief, constraints, and refinement.

❌ Undirected Brief
/imagine prompt: a warrior, detailed, cool
  • Anatomically broken — distorted proportions and hands
  • Completely generic medieval style, no brand differentiation
  • Flat lighting, no emotional direction
  • Required 10+ regenerations — zero consistency
  • No art direction, no campaign application
✅ Directed Creative Brief
/imagine prompt: Cinematic portrait of a cybernetic warrior, intricate obsidian armor with glowing green accents, photorealistic, 8k resolution, volumetric lighting, hyper-detailed --ar 16:9 --v 6.0
  • More specific visual style and mood
  • Precise style and mood matching the creative brief
  • Clearer composition and better prompt control
  • Fewer random outputs and easier refinement
  • More suitable for portfolio, social, or concept use
